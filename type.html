4 Common Skin Types

Normal Skin

Description

Normal skin is balanced—feeling neither too dry nor too oily. It is not prone to breakouts, flakiness, or feeling greasy or tight. However, despite having no specific issues or concerns, it still requires proper skincare to look and feel its best. The ideal normal skin routine helps maintain your skin’s hydration by locking in moisture and supporting your skin’s protective barrier.

Tips for Normal Skin

  • Cleanse twice a day with a gentle, nonfoaming cleanser
  • Moisturize with a light, hydrating moisturizer
  • Protect your skin from sun damage with an SPF of 30 or more

Oily Skin

Description

Oily skin is primarily a result of excessive sebum production. This type of skin has excess oil secretion and is mostly shiny, sweaty, and more prone to suffer from acne, blackheads, etc. Sebum — the oily substance released by sebaceous glands under the skin, and sebaceous glands can change depending on hormones, topical skin care products, and underlying conditions like acne or rosacea.

Tips for Oily Skin

  • Wash your face regularly with warm water and a gentle soap
  • Use face masks that contain clay, honey, or oatmeal
  • Use moisturizers daily after washing

Dry Skin

Description

‘Dry’ is used to describe a skin type that produces less sebum than normal skin. As a result, dry skin is weak in retaining moisture and building a protective moisture shield against external influences. This leads to an impaired moisture barrier function. Dry skin exists in varying degrees of severity, and in different forms that are not always clearly distinguishable.

Tips for Dry Skin

  • Gently wash your face at least twice a day
  • Use allergen-free moisturizing soap
  • Choose fabrics that are kind to your skin

Combination Skin

Description

Combination skin refers to people with both dry and oily areas on the face. It is characterized by non-uniform oil production: more active oil glands in the T-zone (across the forehead and down the bridge of the nose to the tip of the chin) and less active oil glands elsewhere.

Tips for Combination Skin

  • Use a gentle facial cleanser
  • Avoid products with harsh ingredients, such as fragrance or alcohol
  • Use a gentle, non-abrasive, leave-on BHA exfoliant
